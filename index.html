There are two types of asphalt pavement: hot mix and cold mix.

Hot mix is the most common type for paving roads and parking lots. It is made by heating aggregates, such as gravel or sand, to a high temperature before mixing them with bitumen, a sticky black substance that binds the aggregate together. The mixture is then placed on a stone bed and compacted with a steamroller to create a smooth surface.

Cold mix asphalt is not as commonly used as a hot mix but has certain advantages. It can be mixed at lower temperatures, making it ideal for use in cold weather climates. It is also easier to repair than hot mix pavement, which requires special equipment and training to patch.

Asphalt is recyclable, so you can feel good about choosing a sustainable material for your driveway.


There are many reasons why people might choose to install an asphalt driveway. Perhaps they like the blacktop’s look or appreciate its durability and practicality. Whatever the reason, most people don’t overthink about where their asphalt came from or what happens to it when it’s no longer needed. But the truth is, asphalt is recyclable. It’s one of the most recyclable materials around. That means you can feel good about choosing asphalt for your driveway – you’re not only making a sustainable choice, but you’re also helping to keep resources in use for as long as possible.

Asphalt recycling is pretty simple. When old asphalt pavement is removed, it’s crushed into small pieces and then reused in new asphalt. That old asphalt can be recycled and used over and over again, which not only saves resources but also reduces the need for landfill space.

In addition to being recycled, some of the components of asphalt can also be reused. For example, the liquid asphalt binder that holds together the aggregate in the pavement can be recovered and used again. This process is called “reclaimed asphalt pavement” (RAP), a vital part of the asphalt recycling process.
